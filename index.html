    • Process

    • 1. Research

      In order to succeed in the task at hand, I must observe, understand and analyze the particular requirements for the given project or proposal. The research phase includes speaking directly to the client or brand to understand the project audience, goals and messaging.

    • 2. Create Concepts

      The creative phase does not only involve making the product visually appealling, it must also solve the given problems stated during the research phase. My designs are user friendly and intuitive for the users of the product.

    • 3. Revise

      In order to have a successful product, there must be constant communication between myself and the client. After a few concepts have been presented, there is always room for revision. It is not complete until the task is fully answered.

    • 4. Design & Build

      This is where the product comes together. With my sense of technology, design, intuitiveness and interactivity, the product is ready to be brought to life.

    Design Thinking & Process

    Design projects must pass through three spaces; inspiration, for the problem to find solutions; ideation, for generating, developing, and testing ideas that may lead to solutions; and implementation, for the charting of a path to market. Projects will loop back through these spaces more than once as ideas are refined and new directions taken.

    Boston Symphony Orchestra

    Typography II

    Project Outline: Create a poster illustrating the BSO schedule for the season.
    My Take: Organize content by month and day. Be able to read the main concept from far away. As the reader gets closer to the poster, they are able to see that it is a yearly schedule. The thick lines in the background are used to separate months as well as add an attention grabbing element and striking composition.

    Helvetica

    Typography II

    Project Outline: Create a thirty page typography book. Must have a theme and a correlating story.
    My Take: I created a book illustrating the history of Helvetica. What the typefaces initial purpose was and how it came to be one of the most influential and most used typeface to date. By implementing the grid, it allowed for large dramatic imagery and nicely displayed text. Of course using Helvetica throughout the book showed it’s true light and gave it a simplistic, clean and finished look.

    Perks of Being a Wallflower

    Graphic Design I

    Project Outline: Redesign an existing book cover
    My Take: The Perks of Being a Wallflower is one of the most influential book for young adults. It shows the struggles for teenagers in a way that most young adults can relate to. The first person style of writing further illustrates that not all teenagers are alone in the world. I chose to incorporate a typewriter as that is one of the significant symbolic items apparent in the novel. I took this photograph myself to give it an even more personal feel. By placing a post-it note on the classic item is an interesting play on vintage versus modern. The post-it note says “Best Book for Young Adults, 2000”, which gives it an even more outside feel which is an added dimension for young adults who feel like they are of a different world.

    Icon Development

    Graphic Design I

    Project Outline: Choose an every day item and create a simplistic icon
    My Take: I chose a tape dispenser because it has an unusual shape, and one that is recognizable at that. We had to draw this item using different methods as well as take pictures of the item. Doing this allowed us to fully understand the items textures, shapes and use. Making a 3D item 2D is no easy task, but my tape dispenser shows the rigid texture of the metal where the tape is torn, and the smooth plastic texture of the dispenser. It is recognizable of what it is representing.
